The Wealth of Nations

Question: Which countries have the richest people and which have the poorest people?


Data

To answer this question, we can refer to economic indicator data from the World Bank. In particular, we will look at GDP per Capita (since we care about the wealth of individuals, not of the entire nation). Here, the World Bank has provided the GDP per Capita for all countries from 1960 to 2015. Let's limit our dataset to just the year 2014, the most recent (nearly complete) year available.


Heatmap

Note: Here, we display an image of a heatmap from Google Fusion Tables, since Google doesn't allow users to embed actual heatmaps of Geocoded locations.

The heatmap gives us a good idea about the density of individual wealth in various parts of the world. We notice that wealth is highly concentrated in Europe, Arab countries, and a part of China. Many countries in Africa and Asia are poorer.
